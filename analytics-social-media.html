Executive Summary

Social drove $8.4K in Shopify-attributed revenue this month (+34% vs prior period) from 5,640 sessions. Note: this is last-click UTM attribution only — it doesn't capture view-through or assisted conversions, so actual social influence is likely higher. Instagram leads at $5.3K revenue with a 7.8% engagement rate. TikTok followers grew 18%, though engagement rate declined for the 2nd consecutive month. Reels averaged 9.4% engagement — the highest of any format this period.

Worth investigating: Only 4 of 22 Instagram posts currently have product tags. Tagged posts drove 3.2x more sessions per post — if you haven't tested tagging more broadly, it may be worth experimenting with a few of your recent high-reach posts to see if the pattern holds.

Opportunities to Consider
Patterns in your March data that may be worth exploring — not prescriptions
1
Consider testing product tags on more Instagram posts
4 of your 22 Instagram posts this month have product tags. Those 4 tagged posts averaged 3.2x more sessions to your store per post than untagged ones. If you haven't tried tagging more broadly, experimenting with a few high-reach posts could help test whether this pattern holds at scale.
→ View Untagged Posts
2
Reels are significantly outperforming other formats
Your Reels averaged 9.4% engagement vs. 2.8% for static images this month. Reels also reached 5.2x more non-followers. If your content calendar allows, consider whether shifting some static posts to short-form video is feasible — but only if the production effort makes sense for your team.
→ View Format Comparison
3
TikTok audience is growing fast but engagement is softening
TikTok followers grew 18% this month (+752), your fastest-growing channel. But engagement rate declined for the 2nd straight month. This can happen when follower growth outpaces content relevance. It may be worth reviewing which TikTok posts drove the highest engagement to see if there's a content style or topic pattern worth leaning into.
